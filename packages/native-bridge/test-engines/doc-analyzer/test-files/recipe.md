# Classic Sourdough Bread

*A beginner-friendly guide to baking artisan sourdough at home.*

## Ingredients

### For the Levain
- 50g mature sourdough starter
- 50g whole wheat flour
- 50g bread flour
- 100g warm water (78°F)

### For the Dough
- 800g bread flour
- 200g whole wheat flour
- 750g water
- 20g fine sea salt
- 200g levain (from above)

## Instructions

### Day 1: Build the Levain

1. Mix all levain ingredients in a clean jar
2. Cover loosely and let ferment at room temperature for **6-8 hours**
3. The levain is ready when it has doubled in size and passes the *float test*

> **Float test:** Drop a small spoonful of levain into water. If it floats, it's ready. If it sinks, give it more time.

### Day 1: Mix the Dough

1. Combine flours and water in a large bowl — **do not add salt yet**
2. Mix until no dry flour remains
3. Cover and rest for 45 minutes (*autolyse*)
4. Add salt and levain, then pinch and fold until fully incorporated

### Day 1: Bulk Fermentation

This is where the magic happens. Over the next **4-6 hours**, perform a series of stretch-and-folds:

- **Every 30 minutes** for the first 2 hours (4 sets total)
- Then leave undisturbed for the remaining time
- The dough is ready when it has increased by ~50% and feels airy

> **Warning:** Over-fermentation is the most common mistake. Watch the dough, not the clock.

### Day 1: Shape and Cold Proof

1. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface
2. Pre-shape into a round and bench rest for 20 minutes
3. Final shape into a tight boule or batard
4. Place seam-side up in a floured banneton
5. Cover and refrigerate for **12-16 hours**

### Day 2: Bake

1. Preheat oven to **500°F** with a Dutch oven inside — for at least 45 minutes
2. Score the top of the loaf with a sharp blade
3. Bake covered for **20 minutes**
4. Remove lid, reduce to **450°F**, bake for another **20-25 minutes**
5. Cool on a wire rack for at least **1 hour** before cutting

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Dense crumb | Under-fermented | Extend bulk fermentation |
| Too sour | Over-fermented or cold starter | Feed starter more often |
| Flat loaf | Weak shaping | Practice tighter shaping |
| Burned bottom | Oven too hot at base | Place a baking sheet below |

## Notes

This recipe yields one large loaf (~1.8 kg). For two smaller loaves, divide after bulk fermentation.

The `hydration` of this recipe is approximately **75%**, which is moderate. Advanced bakers can push to 80-85% for a more open crumb.

*Happy baking!*
