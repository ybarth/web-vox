Chapter One: The Signal

The radio crackled to life at 3:47 AM.

"Is anyone out there?" The voice was thin, distorted by static, but unmistakably human. Dr. Elena Vasquez leaned forward in her chair, her coffee forgotten.

She pressed the transmit button. "This is Listening Post Bravo. We read you. Identify yourself."

Silence. Then the static shifted, and the voice returned — closer now, clearer.

"My name is Marcus Cole. I've been broadcasting for... I don't know how long. Days, maybe weeks." A pause. "You're the first one to answer."

Elena exchanged a glance with her colleague. The frequency this signal arrived on hadn't been active in over a decade.

"Mr. Cole," she said carefully, "where are you transmitting from?"

Another long pause. When the answer came, Elena's blood ran cold.

"I'm transmitting from Station Aurora. But that's impossible, isn't it? Station Aurora was decommissioned in 2019."

* * *

The morning briefing was tense. Director Harmon stood at the head of the conference table, his face a mask of controlled concern.

"Let me be clear," he said. "Station Aurora was dismantled. The equipment was removed. There is no power source, no antenna array, nothing that could generate a signal."

"And yet," Elena replied, sliding a printout across the table, "here's the spectrograph. It's real. It's coming from Aurora's exact coordinates."

Harmon studied the printout for a long moment. "What do you recommend?"

"Someone has to go out there," Elena said. "Someone has to see what's transmitting."
