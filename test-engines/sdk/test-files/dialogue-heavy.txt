"Good morning!" said Alice, setting down her teacup with a cheerful clink.

"Is it?" muttered the Hatter, not looking up from his newspaper. "I hadn't noticed."

Alice frowned. "You seem rather cross today."

"Cross?" He lowered the paper just enough to peer at her over the top. "I'm not cross. I'm merely efficient with my emotions. Conserving energy."

"For what?"

"For later." He turned a page with exaggerated care. "One never knows when one might need a good tantrum."

The Dormouse yawned from across the table. "He's been like this since Tuesday."

"Since Monday, actually," the Hatter corrected.

"What happened on Monday?" asked Alice.

The Hatter and the Dormouse exchanged a meaningful look.

"We don't talk about Monday," they said in unison.

Alice sighed. "This is going to be one of those conversations, isn't it?"

"Every conversation is one of those conversations," said the Hatter, finally folding his newspaper. "The question is which those you mean."

He paused, then added: "More tea?"
