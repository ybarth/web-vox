# Classic French Onion Soup

## Overview

A rich, deeply flavored soup topped with crusty bread and melted Gruyère cheese. Serves 4–6.

**Prep time:** 20 minutes
**Cook time:** 1 hour 30 minutes
**Total time:** 1 hour 50 minutes

## Ingredients

### For the Soup
- 4 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 cup dry white wine
- 6 cups beef broth
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1 bay leaf

### For the Topping
- 6 slices French bread, toasted
- 2 cups shredded Gruyère cheese
- Fresh thyme for garnish

## Instructions

1. **Caramelize the onions.** Melt butter with olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Add sliced onions and stir to coat. Cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

2. **Low and slow.** Reduce heat to medium-low. Add sugar and salt. Continue cooking for 35–40 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes, until onions are deeply golden brown.

3. **Deglaze.** Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Pour in white wine and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.

4. **Simmer.** Add beef broth, thyme sprigs, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook for 20 minutes.

5. **Assemble.** Ladle soup into oven-safe bowls. Top each with toasted bread and a generous handful of Gruyère.

6. **Broil.** Place bowls under the broiler for 2–3 minutes, until cheese is bubbly and golden.

> **Warning:** Bowls will be extremely hot. Use oven mitts and place on a heat-safe surface.

> **Tip:** For the best caramelized onions, resist the urge to rush. Low, slow heat is the secret.

## Variations

- **Vegetarian:** Substitute mushroom broth for beef broth
- **Gluten-free:** Use gluten-free bread for the croutons
- **Extra depth:** Add a splash of cognac with the wine
