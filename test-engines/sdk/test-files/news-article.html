Breakthrough in Quantum Computing Achieves New Milestone

By Sarah Chen | March 9, 2026 | Science & Technology

Researchers at the National Quantum Laboratory announced today that they have achieved 1,000-qubit quantum coherence for the first time, maintaining stable quantum states for over 47 seconds — shattering the previous record of 12 seconds.

What This Means

The breakthrough could accelerate the development of practical quantum computers by several years. "This changes our timeline completely," said Dr. James Park, lead researcher on the project. "We're now looking at useful quantum applications within 3 to 5 years, not 10 to 15."

"We've crossed a threshold that many believed was still years away. The implications for cryptography, drug discovery, and climate modeling are profound."

— Dr. James Park, National Quantum Laboratory

Key Achievements

  1. Sustained 1,000-qubit coherence for 47.3 seconds
  2. Error rates reduced to 0.01% per gate operation
  3. New cooling system operates at 10 millikelvin
  4. Scalable architecture supporting up to 10,000 qubits

The announcement sent ripples through the technology sector:
